# Research Report: OSM TIGER Audit Pipeline for MetroNow Service Zones in Hamilton County, OH

## TL;DR

- **Via Transportation explicitly uses OpenStreetMap as the base for ViaMapping (the routing layer underneath ViaAlgo) for every microtransit deployment, including Cincinnati MetroNow!** — corrections to OSM in the four MetroNow zones therefore feed directly into Via's routing tiles after their next ingest cycle, even though SORTA itself publishes only fixed-route GTFS/GTFS-RT and no public GTFS-Flex feed for MetroNow. This is the single most important finding because it converts the audit from a generic mapping exercise into a measurable improvement to Hamilton County on-demand transit operations.
- **The 2007–2008 TIGER/Line import (DaveHansenTiger, MTFCC-based) introduced well-documented systematic defects — USPS abbreviation spacing ("Mac Donald St"), stripped apostrophes/diacritics, residential-by-default classification (CFCC A4x → highway=residential), braided dual carriageways with wrong-side oneway tags, over-connected grade-separated nodes, and unreliable `tiger:reviewed=no` tags** — and these defects are demonstrably present in the suburban edges of Hamilton County despite the Cincinnati wiki's claim that "virtually all" TIGER roads have been redrawn. Authoritative ground-truth sources (CAGIS quarterly download, ODOT TIMS Road Inventory, TIGER/Line 2024 vintage `tl_2024_39061_roads`, Hamilton County Auditor parcels) are all openly licensed and can be cross-referenced.
- **The Python pipeline can use the OSM API v0.6 with OAuth 2.0 (OAuth 1.0a was sunset in June 2024) under hard limits of 10,000 elements per changeset (CGImap) and a per-hour edit cap that ramps from 1,000 → 100,000 over a new account's first week** — the safest production architecture is: harvest via Overpass API (`out meta geom`) or Geofabrik US PBF, validate against CAGIS/ODOT, route low-confidence fixes through a MapRoulette challenge (with `mr-cli` cooperative challenges) rather than direct bot edits, and use a dedicated `_cincyimport`-style account that follows the existing Cincinnati community precedent set by the CAGIS building import.

## Key Findings

### Section 1 — Via Transportation / MetroNow data architecture

1. **Via's "ViaMapping" is an OSM-derived custom layer.** Via's own product blog states unambiguously: *"When Via launches a service, our mapping team carefully augments a base map — pulled from Open Street Maps — to reflect our new partner's goals and priorities."* A separate Via post on paratransit confirms: *"Via Paratransit augments standard maps (like OSM) with a proprietary mapping layer, customized for the unique needs and preferences of your paratransit service."* This is the empirical link that justifies the audit project in the four MetroNow zones.
2. **Routing/dispatch is done by ViaAlgo, not by an open routing engine.** Via does not expose OSRM/Valhalla/GraphHopper directly. ViaAlgo consumes the augmented OSM tiles plus learned per-fleet road speeds, agency-defined no-go areas, custom POIs (e.g., bus stops to encourage first/last-mile use), and operator-defined turn restrictions. The base graph topology, however, comes from OSM.
3. **Via's web/dispatcher tooling stack (from Via's public engineering job listings for the Remix-by-Via team):** TypeScript/React + Mapbox GL on the front end; Rails, Python, and PostgreSQL/PostGIS on the back end. Mapbox is used for cartography and likely for some matrix/directions calls, but the operational routing is internal.
4. **Via does not publish GTFS-Flex for its on-demand services.** The Transit app's vendor documentation lists Via among demand-response providers that integrate via a *proprietary API*, alongside Curb, Lyft, Spare, RideCo, Padam, and Uber; MobilityData is steering Via-class providers toward the new GOFS standard (rebranded GOFS-lite, December 2024) but as of May 2026 there is no public GOFS or GTFS-Flex feed for Cincinnati MetroNow!.
5. **What SORTA does publish openly:**
   - GTFS Schedule for fixed-route only: `https://www.go-metro.com/uploads/GTFS/google_transit_info.zip` (Mobility Database mdb-366; updated quarterly per their developer agreement).
   - GTFS-RT (Trapeze TM-GTFS): `tmgtfsprd.sorttrpcloud.com/.../vehicle/vehiclepositions.pb`, `…/tripupdate/tripupdates.pb`, `…/alert/alerts.pb`. MetroNow! is **not** included in any of these feeds.
6. **MetroNow! service footprint and history.** Launched May 2023; 200,000+ trips through the 2025 Blue Ash/Montgomery expansion. The four operational zones — Blue Ash/Montgomery (also serving Deer Park, Silverton, Kenwood, Madeira), Springdale/Sharonville, Northgate/Mt. Healthy, Forest Park/Pleasant Run — all sit in the northern suburban arc of Hamilton County, which is precisely the area where TIGER artifacts have not been thoroughly redrawn (downtown and uptown Cincinnati have been, per the OSM Cincinnati wiki).
7. **Via's data ingest cadence is not publicly documented.** Via does not publish a roadmap for OSM updates into ViaMapping; their materials only state that ViaMapping *"learns from historical road speeds in your service, and uses that information to generate increasingly accurate predictions over time."* This means corrections pushed to OSM will reach Via's tiles whenever the mapping team next refreshes — likely on the order of weeks to a service launch/expansion cycle, but this should be confirmed with SORTA and Via directly.

### Section 2 — TIGER-import defects relevant to Hamilton County

1. **The import that touched Hamilton County was the 2007–2008 TIGER 2005 import by user `DaveHansenTiger`** (Ruby-based, MTFCC switch from earlier CFCCs), with subsequent automated cleanups: a January 2010 superfluous-node-tag cleanup (removed `source=tiger_import_dch_v0.6_20070813`, `tiger:tlid`, `tiger:upload_uuid`, `tiger:county` from nodes) and an emacsen abbreviation-expansion bot in December 2012. The OSM wiki publishes Overpass queries that detect ways untouched since these specific bot runs; the audit pipeline should use those queries verbatim for its baseline scan in the four zones rather than relying on `tiger:reviewed=no`.
2. **`tiger:reviewed=no` is officially deprecated as a quality signal.** The OSM wiki Key:tiger:reviewed page explicitly warns: *"For various reasons, mappers did not follow this procedure consistently enough for the key to mean much on a large scale… developers of tools such as TIGERMap have developed different more reliable ways of seeing areas which need fixup."* The pipeline should either (a) detect `last-edited-by-import-bot AND no subsequent way edits` via Overpass, or (b) compare OSM geometry against TIGER 2024 directly. Consensus has shifted to allowing wholesale removal of `tiger:*` tags as cleanup completes.
3. **Documented systematic TIGER defect classes in the OSM wiki TIGER fixup page (all confirmed against Hamilton County conditions):**
   - **Name field artifacts:** TIGER stored names in ALL CAPS with USPS abbreviations and split across `:fedirp/:fename/:fetype/:fedirs`. Mixed-case prefixes were rendered with a literal space (e.g., "Mac Donald St" instead of "MacDonald Street"); apostrophes and diacritics became spaces ("O Toole Avenue", "Canada Road" for Cañada Road).
   - **Highway-class default bias:** Most A4x CFCCs were defaulted to `highway=residential`. Many should be `highway=unclassified` (rural connectors), `tertiary` (suburban arterials), or `service` (driveways/alleys). The wiki notes "Proposal: A4x would be better as 'unclassified' than as 'residential'."
   - **Oneway errors from braided dual carriageways:** TIGER's "separated" attribute was sometimes lost or rendered as two interlocking ways with oneways pointing in inconsistent directions. (`osm-unbraid` cleaned only a fraction.) These manifest as routing failures where ViaAlgo cannot make an obvious left turn or wrongly thinks a divided arterial is a single carriageway.
   - **Over-connectedness at grade separations:** TIGER had no z-level concept, so freeway/overpass intersections were collapsed into shared nodes during the import. This is the canonical "routing from a freeway onto an overpass" bug.
   - **Excess nodes on rural ways:** Should be passed through JOSM "Simplify Way" (or osmium-equivalent) before redrawing.
   - **Incorrect highway-link tagging:** Motorway_link improperly tagged as motorway, and ref-tag inconsistency ("I 5" vs "I-5" vs "I5") — important for MetroNow! zones because all four zones touch I-275, I-75, I-71, or US-22/SR-126.
4. **Greater Cincinnati–specific corroboration.** A discussion on the OSM Cincinnati wiki (`User_talk:Vid_the_Kid`) by Minh Nguyễn documents that "in Butler County (Hamilton area), a few of us have been busy correcting every single way in the county. It looks like someone was drunk when adding that data to TIGER!" and that user `RSatterf` separately re-imported all of Greene County, leading to duplicate ways and stray "loose" nodes — i.e., parts of the broader Cincinnati metro have a *second* layer of pollution beyond the DaveHansenTiger import. Hamilton County's *outer* suburban townships (which is what MetroNow! serves) are the most likely to retain unreviewed TIGER segments, despite the Cincinnati wiki's general claim that Tri-State TIGER roads have largely been redrawn.
5. **Other bulk imports/edits that have touched Hamilton County:**
   - **CAGIS Building Import** (ongoing since 2018, formal proposal by Nate Wessel, Bogdan Petrea/Telenav, and Minh Nguyễn): ~358,167 building footprints from CAGIS, with addresses pulled from the parcel layer. Phase 1 (~295,000 non-conflicting buildings) is complete; Phase 2 (manual conflation of ~62,000 overlapping buildings) was started December 25, 2021 and is ongoing. Tasking-manager project 107 on tasks.openstreetmap.us. Importers used `_cincyimport`-suffix accounts.
   - No NHD/USGS hydrography import is documented as having significant impact on the four service zones beyond standard TIGER linear water.
6. **Authoritative ground-truth datasets for Hamilton County (FIPS 39061):**
   - **TIGER/Line 2024:** `tl_2024_39061_roads.zip` from the Census Bureau (released September 26, 2024; updates through May 2024). 2025 vintage's "Streets Digital Map Database" augments fullname fields and address ranges. Diffing OSM against TIGER 2024 will reveal both genuine new roads and legacy import drift.
   - **CAGIS Open Data Hub** (`data-cagisportal.opendata.arcgis.com`): quarterly updates, public-domain-equivalent license (City of Cincinnati declared the building/parcel layers PD; CAGIS requires a disclaimer + credit). Layers include real-time address-and-street centerlines maintained as part of permitting workflow — substantially more current than TIGER. Accuracy: meets National Map Accuracy Standards for 1"=100' base map, NAD83.
   - **ODOT TIMS Road Inventory** (`tims.dot.state.oh.us`): the state's official certified-mileage source for HPMS reporting; annual update. Documented on the OSM wiki "ODOT TIMS" page as no-copyright per ODOT GISP Ian Kidner's email. Includes attributes (lane miles, federal-aid eligibility, speed zones, NHS) that TIGER lacks. A community member (`mblaine`) publishes Mapnik tiles of ODOT data at `https://tiles.mblaine.com/ohio/{z}/{x}/{y}.png` — usable as a JOSM TMS background to confirm TIGER name corrections.
   - **Hamilton County Auditor GIS** (parcels) — useful for address verification but the buildings are now mostly imported.
7. **The Cincinnati OSM community is active and CAGIS-aware.** Local mappers (Minh Nguyễn / "Vid the Kid", Nate Wessel) have run community discussions about TIGER cleanup, are on first-name terms with CAGIS staff, and have established the `_cincyimport` account convention. Any mechanical edit by this audit pipeline should be coordinated with them on the OSM US Slack and `community.openstreetmap.org`.

### Section 3 — Claude Code + OSM technical capabilities

1. **OSM API v0.6 hard limits the pipeline must respect (verified May 2026):**
   - **Authentication:** OAuth 2.0 only. OAuth 1.0a and HTTP Basic Auth were shut down in **June 2024**. Use Authorization Code + PKCE flow; the wiki maintains a complete worked example. Required scope for editing is `write_api`; `write_changeset_comments` exists separately.
   - **Changeset size:** CGImap (the production OSM endpoint) limits a single changeset to **10,000 elements / 50 MB diff (uncompressed)**. The Rails port allows 50,000 historically, but production traffic is on CGImap.
   - **Per-account rate limit (added November 2023):** 1,000 changes/hour for new accounts in the first day, ramping non-linearly to 100,000/hour over a week. Reported users drop to a 100/hour floor. A dedicated "import" role with elevated limits exists; the DWG can grant it on request and is the appropriate channel for this project. The current limit can be polled at `/api/0.6/capabilities`.
   - **Tag-value length:** 255 Unicode codepoints (not bytes) for keys, values, role strings.
2. **OAuth 2.0 + Python:** The osmapi PyPI package (Etienne Chové's library, now community-maintained) supports OAuth 2.0 via `oauthcli` or `requests-oauthlib`. The `auth = OpenStreetMapAuth(client_id, client_secret, ['read_prefs','write_api']).auth_code()` pattern is the standard. A non-trivial gotcha: when redirecting via web UI, use `https://` for callbacks (only `http://127.0.0.1` is allowed unencrypted); for non-web Python scripts, the special URI `urn:ietf:wg:oauth:2.0:oob` is the correct redirect.
3. **Bulk-harvest comparison for the four zone bboxes:**

   | Method | Pros | Cons | Recommended for |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | Overpass API (`out meta geom`) | Tag-filtered, includes user/version/timestamp metadata, JSON output, free public servers | Hard to anticipate response size; `recurse` semantics tricky; Overpass instances rate-limit large queries | Defect-class scanning (e.g., `way[tiger:reviewed=no]({{bbox}})`, last-edited-by-DaveHansenTiger queries) |
   | OSM `/api/0.6/map?bbox=` | Simple, returns full referenced nodes | Hard 0.25° × 0.25° bbox limit; meant for editor downloads, not analytics | Last-mile fetch right before edit submission |
   | Geofabrik US extract | 11.1 GB PBF as of May 2026; daily diffs; free; works with osmium/imposm/osm2pgsql | Strips user/uid/changeset metadata (EU GDPR); for full metadata you must log in to Geofabrik with an OSM account and use the *internal* server | Bulk PostGIS load, building county-wide change-detection indexes |
   | Planet OSM diffs (minutely/hourly/daily) | Streaming detection of new TIGER-import-vintage edits planet-wide | Overkill for one county; processing burden | Continuous monitoring after initial audit |

   **Recommendation:** Use Overpass for defect classification (the wiki publishes a ready-made query that returns `way(user:"DaveHansenTiger")(if: timestamp() > "2007-08-03" && timestamp() < "2008-05-04")` filtered to the bbox), Geofabrik for the PostGIS reference snapshot, and the OSM API only for write-back.
4. **MapRoulette is the safest path for non-trivial corrections.** MapRoulette explicitly "encourages careful human attention to each task, in the spirit of OpenStreetMap's Automated Edits code of conduct." The pipeline can:
   - POST a Project + Challenge + batched Tasks through the documented Swagger API at `/docs/swagger-ui/index.html` on `maproulette.org`.
   - Source tasks from either an Overpass query (read-only after task generation — clone-and-recreate to change it) or a GeoJSON feed produced by the Python pipeline.
   - Use `mr-cli` to generate cooperative challenges that include task attachments (e.g., side-by-side OSM-vs-CAGIS imagery).
   - Constrain challenges to the four MetroNow zones via a GeoJSON polygon AOI.
5. **OSMCha for monitoring.** OSMCha is now an OSM US charter project (since February 2023). Its REST API (`osmcha.org/api/v1/`) exposes filtered changesets as JSON and as RSS — the audit's web UI should subscribe to a saved filter for the four bboxes so reviewers see the impact of the pipeline's own edits and any concurrent edits in the same zones. The `osmcha` Python package (PyPI, OSMCha/osmcha) flags suspicious changesets locally; thresholds (`create_threshold=200`, `modify_threshold=200`, `top_threshold=1000`) are tunable.
6. **Existing OSM-related MCP servers compatible with Claude Code (May 2026):**
   - **`jagan-shanmugam/open-streetmap-mcp`** — Python; geocoding, route planning, POI search, neighborhood analysis. Configurable in `claude mcp add-json osm-mcp-server '{"command":"uvx","args":["osm-mcp-server"]}'`.
   - **`NERVsystems/osmmcp`** — Go; explicit, configurable rate limiting (`--nominatim-rps 1.0 --overpass-rps 0.033 --osrm-rps 1.67`); unifies Nominatim, Overpass, OSRM behind one MCP. **This is the strongest option for the audit pipeline** because it ships with rate limits that already comply with public Overpass etiquette.
   - **`wiseman/osm-mcp`** — Python + PostgreSQL/PostGIS; spawns a local Leaflet web viewer; useful for the Express.js UI's preview layer.
   - **Mapbox MCP DevKit** (beta, all Mapbox accounts) — useful for token/style management and bbox/coordinate utilities, but does *not* edit OSM.
   - There is **no public MCP server today that directly performs authenticated OSM API v0.6 writes** — that piece will need to be built into the project's own osm package and exposed as a custom MCP tool. The Claude Code custom-tool docs describe stdio/HTTP transports and `.claude.json`-based registration.
7. **Claude Code can also use Skills/Subagents.** A "PostGIS Geospatial Development" Claude Code Skill (mcpmarket.com) covers SRID 4326/3857/4978 selection, idempotent migrations, and spatial-index optimization — directly relevant for the audit's PostGIS reference snapshot. Long-running stateful sessions (per Anthropic's Long-Running Sessions pattern) suit the conflation pipeline better than per-request invocations.
8. **OSM Automated Edits / Mechanical Edits / Import Guidelines requirements that govern this project (NOT optional):**
   - Document the planned edits at `wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Automated_edits/<username>` *before* starting.
   - Discuss on `talk-us@`/`community.openstreetmap.org` and on local channels (Cincinnati maintains an OSM US Slack presence and the `Cincinnati, Ohio` OSM wiki page).
   - Use a dedicated import account (e.g., `metronow_audit_import` or `<your_username>_cincyimport` to follow local convention) with a wiki link in the profile.
   - Tag every changeset with: `comment=`, `source=`, `bot=yes` if applicable, `mechanical=yes` if applicable, and `description=<URL of wiki page documenting the edit>`.
   - Honor opt-out requests immediately.
   - Keep changesets **well below** the 10,000-element CGImap limit (community norm is ~500 for mechanical edits to keep diff review tractable in OSMCha).
   - Be prepared for revert without notice if the community objects, regardless of process compliance.

## Caveats

1. **Via's actual OSM ingest cadence is not publicly documented.** Statements that corrections "feed directly into Via's routing tiles after their next ingest cycle" are inferences from Via's own description of how ViaMapping is built, not a confirmed timeline. A direct conversation with SORTA technical staff and Via's mapping team is the only way to confirm whether OSM edits propagate weekly, monthly, or only at zone-launch milestones.
2. **The Cincinnati OSM wiki's claim that "Virtually all roads imported from TIGER have been redrawn" is at least partly aspirational.** It is well-documented for downtown and uptown Cincinnati but the suburban arc that MetroNow! actually serves is the area where TIGER artifacts most plausibly persist. The audit pipeline should treat the four MetroNow bboxes as candidate-rich until the first scan confirms or refutes that.
3. **OSM rate-limiting is opaque.** The official documentation explicitly warns: *"the way in which quotas are calculated may change at any time without prior notice."* The 1,000→100,000 ramp curve described in PR #4319 is current as of the linked PR but may have been adjusted; always check `/api/0.6/capabilities` at runtime rather than hard-coding values.
4. **The 10,000-element CGImap changeset limit is a *production* limit; the underlying Rails port allows 50,000.** Different OSM API endpoints behave differently. Code defensively to the 10,000 number.
5. **MapRoulette's overpass-query field is read-only after task generation.** If the audit defect-detection algorithm evolves, you must clone the challenge rather than edit it. Plan for challenge rotation.
6. **OAuth 1.0a tutorials are obsolete.** A great deal of older Python OSM tooling on the open web (including Stack Overflow answers and pre-2024 Medium posts) still describes OAuth 1.0a flows that no longer work as of June 2024. Filter aggressively for post-mid-2024 documentation.
7. **CAGIS data is "public domain–like" but requires a disclaimer + credit.** The CAGIS GIS Download terms require acknowledging the disclaimer and providing a "data creator credit." Bake this into the changeset `source=` tag and the project's web UI footer to stay compliant.
8. **ODOT TIMS license language is permissive but qualified.** ODOT's "no warranty" disclaimer means the road inventory should not be used as a sole authoritative source — cross-reference with CAGIS and aerial imagery before pushing classification changes.
9. **No public MCP server currently writes to OSM.** Building this capability into the project's own osm package as a custom MCP tool is unavoidable; treat the existing OSM MCPs (NERVsystems/osmmcp, jagan-shanmugam/open-streetmap-mcp, wiseman/osm-mcp) as *read-side* infrastructure only.
10. **The Cincinnati Metro Authority article cited at one point in the search results (cincinnatimetroauthority.com) is a non-official aggregator site, not SORTA.** Cite go-metro.com or sorta-issued press releases when documenting MetroNow! technical details to avoid sourcing from unofficial summaries.
11. **Some research questions could not be definitively answered from public sources.** Specifically: (a) Via's data ingestion cadence for OSM updates; (b) Via's internal data-quality validation steps; (c) whether MetroNow! routing has been measurably affected by specific Hamilton County OSM defects. These are best answered through direct outreach to SORTA's IT/Innovation group and Via's deployment engineering team for the Cincinnati account, not from public web sources.
